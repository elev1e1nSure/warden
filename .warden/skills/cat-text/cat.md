Ancient Egyptians revered cats not just as pets but as protectors of the home, believing they brought good luck and warded off evil spirits. A cat's tail can tell you a lot about its mood — a high, quivering tail usually means excitement or affection, while a puffed-up tail signals fear or agitation. Despite their reputation for aloofness, domestic cats form strong social bonds with their owners and can recognize their voice from a stranger's.
